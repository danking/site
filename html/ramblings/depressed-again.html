Depressed Again

I seem to have recurring feelings of sadness. It might be tied to my semi–voluntary solitude. I describe myself, with some general reservations about labels, as an introvert.

I experience periods, usually lasting a few months, during which I feel a malaise. Throughout this essay, I'll use the word "ordinarily" to mean "at times other than these periods of malaise"; however, the frequency and duration of these malaise–periods makes them seem as if they are in fact ordinary and times of happiness are extraordinary.

Ordinarily, I enjoy solitude. In solitude, I am: most productive, most at ease, most clear of mind: most conscious.

However, for multiple–month–long stretches, malaise permeates my life. When I eat, while I lie in bed awaiting sleep, when I work, and when I try to relax, a latent sense of dissatisfaction with my lot in life is present.

This feeling compels me to incessantly check social media, my email, and news sites. It seems that I am either distracting myself from this unsettling feeling or desperately seeking social contact. Perhaps, I am doing both.

My reactions to feelings of malaise disrupt my life. I find it difficult to focus on work, personal projects, and even relaxation. I cannot sit through a whole movie, watch a TV show, or read a book; I am too distracted by this dissatisfaction. At work and while working on my personal projects, I am frustrated by this feeling and must exert more effort than usual simply to stay on–task.

I am not certain that my social isolation is to blame, but I cannot hide the reality of it. I have co–workers with whom I am friendly. We eat lunch together nearly every day and occasionally eat dinner together. Most nights, I return home and attempt to work on personal projects, work on my undergraduate thesis, or relax by reading a book. I rarely have sufficient fortitude to surmount my malaise and do these things. I spend most weekends the same way: locked in a struggle with my dissatisfaction, desperately trying to be productive.

I do not have any confidants on the continent in which I live, but I have a couple confidants in Boston. I occasionally converse with them, but I feel disconnected from them, out of touch. I am not convinced that distance is the primary problem.

I believe that I, in general, have trouble maintaining friendships. I also have trouble creating friendships. I understand that I am unusual and thus difficult to befriend. I seek out mellow, introverted people: talking with particularly outgoing people is stressful for me. I can temporarily or infrequently cope with them, but I generally avoid such personalities. Unfortunately, I also avoid social groups comprising extroverted personalities; the stress of even one can overwhelm the satisfaction of other friendships.
