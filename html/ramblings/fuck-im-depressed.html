Fuck, I'm Depressed

Defining happiness in terms of success is folly.

Framing your unhappiness as a mistake that you just need to fix doesn't seem helpful.

Be wary of collecting trophies. Acquiring externally defined success leads to regret.

Leaving everything, disappearing; it sounds romantic. Think about it. Realize I'll never survive.

Is life what you thought you were promised?

Twenty-one years in; the mental barriers were built with concrete and they've dried. Tiny mistakes, disappointments, slights, and failures of childhood grow like a bush: thick, impassible, no apparent root.

Did they lie?

Atmospheric darkness pressures the psychological twilight into night. The music commiserates ... does the concert's commotion block the exits? Do the pyrotechnics burn the remaining shreds to ashes?

Did you mishear?

Dust off Maslow again. Food? Check. Water? Check. Air? Check. Third World Problems. Healthy? Check plus. Financial security? Despite loans, I must concede a check; thanks Wall Street & Silicon Valley. Friendship? Erm. Love? Are there half checks? Maybe I could use some social skills. Confidence? Hah. Self-esteem? Hah. Achievement. Not funny anymore. Blech

What promise? Fucking nostalgia.

Passion? Meh. Nothing seems to matter; I can't bring myself to care. The insurmountable piles of work and effort necessary. Augh. Aurelius would be disappointed in passion anyway.

Another six months I'll be unknown.

Plaguing the world with your sadness is cruel to others. Throw it away. Bottle it up. Get rid of it. Nobody wants to read shit writing. Stop filling the internet with dross..

Religion? Hah, the masses have many opiates.

Modern life seems a bit like watching porn. For little work we can have instant pleasure. The delta from instant noodles to cheap-place-down-the-street noodles to real noodles is insignificant. Why work for the best when just OK is within reach? Maximum pleasure minimum pain? Seems wrong though. Food, toys, partying, drunkenness doesn't bring happiness. Bacchus is a fool.

And yet we'll keep going, won't we buddy? You and I. We hate the train we're on, but jumping off looks too painful. It's time to restore the facade, put on the mask, return to life, return to work. Grit your teeth and move on. As long as there's something to appear busy working on, you're fine. Your meager outputs will keep those above happy. Money. Status. They'll come piecemeal. Never enough to upset those on Olympus. Enough to keep you on the train. Our great success is always a month away. After high school. After college. After the loans are paid. Which is, to say, never.

Mortgage youth to acquire the keys that open the never ending series of doors. You'll never be happy. That's the human condition. No pile of self-help books. No religion. No philosophy. This personality has been burned in, positive thinking is hopeless, cheap lies about afterlife are meaningless, steps to happiness are steps to nowhere.
