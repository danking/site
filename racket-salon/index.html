Racket Salon Boston

Racket Salon Boston is a meet up for folks interested in Racket and functional programming. Each meeting consists of a couple talks, chit-chat, and a group outing for dinner.

The atmosphere is open to people of all skill levels and interests. We are eager to meet students, professionals, hobbyists, and other interested individuals! To keep up to date, you should subscribe to our calendar.

We're looking for speakers for future meetings to share with us their stories of Racket hacks, frustrations, and joys. If you're interested in sharing some wisdom, please contact Dan King.

The next meeting will likely be held in late January. Stay tuned to the racket-users mailing list, our calendar (iCal, web), or this website for more news!

The February Meeting

The third meeting of Racket Salon Boston will be held on Thursday February 20th, 2014 at 7pm in Cambridge at Harvard University, Maxwell Dworkin, Room 119 (which is on the second floor).

The confirmed talks are:

  • Mimi Lin will discuss a 3D version of the current HtDP Image and Universe library (2htdp/image and 2htdp/universe); which are commonly used in courses based off the ProgramByDesign project. [ website ]
  • Scott Moore will discuss Shill; a secure shell language built in/on Racket. [ website ]

The January Meeting

We held the second meeting of Racket Salon on Monday January 27, 2013 at Northeastern University, West Village H, Room 366!

We had three talks in January:

  • Vincent St-Amour spoke about the what, the why, and the WAT of the Typed Racket numeric tower. [ slides | website ]
  • Spencer Florence spoke about rkt-http a library designed to make http a little easier. [ rkt-http repo | website ]
  • Jonathan Schuster discussed and demoed his new IRC client library for Racket that lets you easily develop your own IRC chatbot or full-fledged client. [ racket-irc repo | website ]

The December Meeting

The inaugural meeting of Racket Salon Boston was held on the Ninth of December from at Northeastern University in West Village H, Room 366.

We had three talks at the inaugural meeting:

  • Dan King spoke about his experience using Racket for machine learning. [ slides | website ]
  • Greg Hendershott spoke about his #lang rackjure package. [ slides | website ]
  • Asumu Takikawa demoed how to use Typed Racket with classes & objects. [ blog post | website ]
