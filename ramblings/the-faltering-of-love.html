The Faltering of Love

She and I connect better than I've ever connected with someone else before. We both love mathematics. We're bleeding-heart liberals and feminists. She's not turned off by my predilection for Romanticism; in fact, she carries the torch for the traditionally masculine traits that I, at times, eschew.

There's a sort of smoke in the air. Our relationship, it twirls in circles and tangents, ups and downs. It wraps around our legs and arms as we dance the waltz of a new love. The tender smell: unique and fresh, like the fragrance of a luscious fruit picked from a ragged cliff-side.

Ephemeral?

I thought not.

A great distance, at first only physical--easily traversed with bits--is now also emotional. Grasping at the smoke, which is now quickly dissipating, is futile. It was never a constant cloud, but a growing and evolving aura that fed on those things we shared.

I weep ... but life flows on.
